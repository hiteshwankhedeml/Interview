# 🟢 Dot Product Similarity

* <mark style="color:purple;background-color:purple;">**Higher dot product = higher similarity**</mark>
* <mark style="color:purple;background-color:purple;">**Dot product is influenced by vector magnitude**</mark>
* <mark style="color:purple;background-color:purple;">**Dot product multiplies and sums corresponding vector components to measure directional similarity.**</mark>
*   A = \[0.2, 0.5, 0.3]\
    B = \[0.4, 0.1, 0.5]

    Calculation:

    (0.2×0.4)+(0.5×0.1)+(0.3×0.5)(0.2×0.4) + (0.5×0.1) + (0.3×0.5)(0.2×0.4)+(0.5×0.1)+(0.3×0.5) =0.08+0.05+0.15= 0.08 + 0.05 + 0.15=0.08+0.05+0.15 =0.28= 0.28=0.28

    Dot product = **0.28**
